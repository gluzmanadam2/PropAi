The Problem

Property management hasn't changed in forty years. The economics have.

Traditional property managers charge 8–10% of gross rents, operate on business-hours schedules, and rely on manual processes that lead to missed inquiries, delayed maintenance, and opaque reporting.

Self-managing owners save on fees but sacrifice their time — fielding tenant calls at midnight, coordinating vendors between meetings, chasing late rent on weekends.

Neither model scales. We built a third option.

Scope of Service
01

Tenant Communication

Every inbound message — maintenance requests, rent questions, noise complaints, lease inquiries, move-out notices — answered within 60 seconds, at any hour. No voicemail. No missed texts. No delays.

02

Maintenance Coordination

Requests triaged by urgency. Emergencies dispatched within minutes to the highest-rated available vendor. Standard issues queued, assigned, and tracked through completion. Owners approve costs above their set threshold.

03

Rent Collection & Enforcement

Structured reminder sequences escalating from courtesy notice to formal demand. Late fees applied per lease terms. Payment plan inquiries handled and routed. Legal notices drafted and queued for owner approval.

04

Vendor Management

Performance tracking across response time, cost, and tenant satisfaction. Top-performing vendors dispatched first. Insurance certificates monitored. Invoices reconciled against completed work orders.

05

Leasing & Placement

Vacant units syndicated to listing platforms. Inquiries answered instantly with availability and unit details. Applications collected, screened, and presented with a recommendation for owner decision.

06

Financial Reporting

Real-time income and expense tracking. Monthly owner statements generated automatically. Year-end packages organized by Schedule E category for direct handoff to your accountant.
